Kannada What?


A land of rich culture and heritage, India has made significant contributions to Astronomy. Although IAU has accepted many Greek and Arabic names for the celestial bodies in the sky, these celestial bodies have names given by various Indian astronomers and scientists too.

Prof. R.L. Narasimhaiah, in his book '‡≤®‡≤ï‡≥ç‡≤∑‡≤§‡≥ç‡≤∞ ‡≤¶‡≤∞‡≥ç‡≤∂‡≤®', made an attempt to 'rename' the modern constellations with Indian equivalent words and characters from the Indian Mythology.

In addition to this, Dr. Shylaja of Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium, Bengaluru and Dr. Venkateshwara Pai of IISER, Pune, have published several papers decoding names of stars from various ancient Indian scriptures, sculptures and other records.

So this Sky Culture for Stellarium is an initiative to put all these efforts together and bring the night sky to everyone with a curious mind... in Kannada.

Install


  1. Download the latest release from Stellarium Kannada Starlore from @atulbhats's Repository.
  2. Extract the Kannada folder into 
    C:\Program Files\Stellarium\skycultures\
    .
  3. Open Stellarium
  4. Open the Sky and Vieweing Options [F4].
  5. Under the Starlore Tab, select "Kannada".
  6. Explore the Celestial Sphere in Kannada

Celestial Sphere in Kannada

The Kannada Starlore labels all the Constellations with their Kannada Names. Stars and Asterisms with recorded Kannada names are also labelled so.
